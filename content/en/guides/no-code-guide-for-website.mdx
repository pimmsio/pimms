**No-code website tracking** lets you monitor every conversion across your entire website ecosystem without writing any code. The Pimms dashboard includes interactive setup wizards for each supported platform — Webflow, Framer, WordPress, and more — handling all technical details for you.

Whether you built your site with a no-code tool, an AI builder, or a vibe-coded MVP, Pimms gives you complete **conversion attribution** across forms, bookings, and payments. See which campaigns generate actual results, not just page views.

Most no-code platforms have basic analytics but lack one critical piece: **attribution**. You can see that 50 people submitted your form this week, but you have no idea which campaign brought them. Pimms fills that gap.

## Why use Pimms for no-code website tracking?

- **One dashboard for all your conversions.** Your Webflow site has forms, your Cal.com handles bookings, and Stripe processes payments. Instead of checking three separate tools, Pimms gives you one unified view showing which campaigns generate leads, meetings, and sales across all of them.

- **No developer required.** Every Pimms integration has a guided setup wizard. You don't need to write code, configure APIs, or understand webhooks. The wizard walks you through each step and verifies everything works before you're done.

- **Works with any platform that allows custom code.** If your website builder lets you add JavaScript to the head section, Pimms can track conversions on it. This includes Webflow, Framer, WordPress, Bubble, Wix, Squarespace, and most AI-generated apps.

## Prerequisites

- A **Pimms account** ([sign up here](https://app.pimms.io))
- A no-code website or app that allows adding custom code (most paid plans do)
- At least one conversion point — a form, booking page, or payment checkout

## What you'll track

- **Form submissions** — Leads from Webflow, Framer, Elementor, Tally, and other form tools
- **Meeting bookings** — Appointments from Cal.com, Calendly, and Brevo Meetings
- **Payments and sales** — Revenue from Stripe, Podia, and Systeme.io
- **Full attribution** — Which campaign, ad, social post, or referral source drove each conversion

## How to set it up

Go to your [Pimms dashboard](https://app.pimms.io) and choose the setup wizard for your platform. Each integration has a guided step-by-step flow that handles script installation, webhook configuration, and verification. Most integrations take under 5 minutes to set up.

## How it works

1. **Pick your integration** in the Pimms dashboard — the wizard detects what type of tracking you need
2. **Follow the guided steps** — typically: install a script, configure a webhook or OAuth, and verify
3. **Create Pimms links** to your no-code site pages — these are the tracked URLs you'll share
4. **Share your links** in campaigns — ads, emails, LinkedIn, YouTube, etc.
5. **Pimms tracks every conversion** — when a visitor from your link submits a form, books a meeting, or makes a purchase, Pimms attributes it to the campaign

## FAQs

::: faq

### Which no-code platforms support Pimms tracking?

Pimms works with any no-code platform that allows custom code in the head section, including Webflow, Framer, Bubble, Wix, and most AI website builders. Some integrations (like Calendly and Stripe) use OAuth and work regardless of your website platform.

:::

::: faq

### Do I need separate tracking for each tool I use?

Not always. The Pimms detection script is installed once on your website and works with all supported tools. However, some integrations (Stripe, Calendly, Brevo) have their own setup wizards that configure additional connections. Each wizard in the dashboard handles everything for you.

:::

::: faq

### Can I track conversions across different pages and funnels?

Yes! Pimms tracks visitors across your entire no-code site. Whether someone fills a form on page 1 and books a meeting on page 3, you'll see the complete conversion path with full attribution back to the original campaign.

:::

::: faq

### What if my no-code platform isn't listed?

If your platform allows adding JavaScript to the page head, you can likely use the Pimms detection script. Check the [analytics script reference guide](/articles/pimms-analytics-script-complete-guide) for advanced configuration options, or contact support for help with unlisted platforms.

:::
